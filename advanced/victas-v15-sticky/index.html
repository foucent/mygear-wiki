    Victas V15 Sticky (V15s)

    Short court notes on Victas V15 Sticky Max (52.5°)—semi-tacky topsheet over a lively German-style cake sponge. Marketed as an all-around sticky tensor for today’s ball; usable FH or BH.

    Specs (tested Max)
    Hardness (rated) 52.5°
    Uncut weight ~72.4 g
    Cut weight ~50 g (a bit heavier than Butterfly D09c)
    Hardness feel Roughly like DHS Hurricane 3 ~39° sponge feel

    Selling points

    • 2025-era semi-tacky + high-elasticity cake sponge combo
    • Aimed at balanced spin + spring, not a pure china clone

    Forehand

    • Drive: Transparent and crisp; elasticity sits between Tibhar K2 Pro and Stiga DNA Dragon Grip. Clear “sink” into an active sponge.
    • Loop: Low barrier, easy to bottom out, forgiving. Absolute top-end speed/spin average; arc stabilizes as you add force. Needs more forward engagement.
    • Defense: Softish sponge but enough rebound—room to neutralize heavy loops.

    Backhand vs Butterfly D09c

    V15s D09c
    Mass / feel Slightly heavier; sponge more lively More solid; sharper direction
    Short game A bit more tack—safer push/touch Precise, sponge-led power
    High-pressure loop Can feel slightly hollow at the ceiling—meet early, follow through Still the higher top end

    Buy summary

    Feature V15s D09c
    Core idea Topsheet + sponge as one control package Topsheet control + sponge power
    Feel Active, deep dwell, forgiving Solid, very precise
    Power ceiling Medium Very high
    Value Strong for the price Premium price

    Verdict: Not a 1:1 D09c clone. V15s wins on transparent feel and value. Good pick if you want FH (or mild BH) sticky-tensor control with German spring—without D09c money. Pair expectations: mid ceiling, high playability.

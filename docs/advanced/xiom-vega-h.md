# XIOM Vega H (Red V Dual)

**Vega H** (Red V Dual, **50°**) and **Vega Pro H** (Silver V Dual, **47.5°**) use “Dual” in the same sense as many “Hybrid” labels: **semi-tacky European / German tensor**.

FH-oriented Vega H: Max ~**2.2 mm**, uncut ~**77 g**, cut ~**52–53 g**—heavy. Street price around **¥180** at the time of notes.

Semi-tacky topsheet with a dense, “moist” face feel; packaging looks premium.

---

## On-court notes

| Topic | Impression |
| --- | --- |
| **Arc & power** | Long arc. Ceiling near **39–40° Battle II** (blue sponge)—not especially punchy. |
| **Hardness** | Feels near **H3 39–40°**, but thick/heavy so hard to bottom out; little crisp metallic pop. |
| **Touch** | Muffled / “fleshy,” low transparency. Semi-tack helps control. “Biting” sponge, not crisp-hard—harder to punch through than **DNA Dragon Grip** despite the rating. |
| **Spin** | Tack near Vega China / Dragon Grip; denser sponge. Spin between **H3** and **Vega China**—solid mid pack. |
| **Speed** | Tacky face + dense sponge → medium-slow among Euro tensors; Battle II-like, not a speed demon even at full swing. |

---

## Blade pairing reality

On a fast blade (e.g. Ma Lin Carbon–class): without real force it can feel **dead**. Once you load it, arc goes long and flat, yet the sponge still resists clean bottom-out—disappointing for many FH users.

Interestingly it can feel **better on BH** for flick / dwell, but thickness and weight make BH tiring.

!!! tip "If you still want the series"
    Prefer **Vega Pro H (Silver V Dual, 47.5°)** on BH. **Vega H (Red V)** is **not** highly recommended as a first pick.

Notes are subjective; batch and blade change the story.
